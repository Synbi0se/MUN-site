Charles de Gaulle High School

International Section and French International Baccalaureate (IFB):

Since the 2023 academic year, an international section has been opened, allowing students to follow a bilingual path, with specific instruction in English, including history-geography and world knowledge. Students can enroll directly in the tenth grade, while those who have not followed this path must pass language tests.

Football Section:

In collaboration with the Chaumont Football Club, a football section has been set up for students in general, technological, and vocational streams. Students are selected through physical tests and academic records. This section aims to raise the level of sports practice, train students in refereeing, and prepare them for regional competitions.

Creation and Culture Design (CCD):

This technological option is available from the second year of high school and focuses on the theoretical and practical study of design and the arts. Students develop their curiosity, critical thinking, and foundational knowledge of design culture, while exploring experimental design practices. This option is recommended for students interested in the arts, creativity, and teamwork.

Physical Education and Sports Option (EPS):

Since 2000, this option has offered students an additional three hours of weekly instruction beyond the common program. It combines the practice of various sports (such as athletics, swimming, and team sports) with reflection on these activities, addressing topics like health, dietetics, and sports psychology. The option is ideal for those considering further studies in sports or recreation.

European section - Sciences in English :

This section allow students to deepen their language skills while studying scientific subjects in English. Students can earn the European mention on their high school diploma under certain conditions. These sections promote international mobility and cultural openness, while preparing students for an international career.

Options Sciences de l’Ingénieur et Création Innovation Technologiques (SI/CIT) :

This option, indicated during administrative registration, offers students the opportunity to engage in a scientific approach by tackling challenges and carrying out innovative projects. The themes addressed include mobility, eco-design, and product engineering. Students develop their creativity, capacity for innovation, and ability to solve complex problems.
